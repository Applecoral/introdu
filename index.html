Basic Badminton Concepts

Badminton is a fast‑paced racquet sport where players hit a feathered “shuttlecock” back and forth over a high net. Played as singles or doubles, the goal is to land the shuttle in your opponent’s court. Matches use rally scoring to 21 points, demanding agility, precision, and strategy. We will explore three basic concepts:

The Serve

  1. Always hit underhand below the waist and send the shuttle diagonally into your opponent’s service box.
  2. A legal serve starts the rally and must clear the short service line.

Basic Shots

  1. Clear: A high, deep shot to push your opponent to the back court.
  2. Drop: A soft shot that just clears the net and falls quickly.
  3. Smash: A powerful, downward shot to finish the rally.

Scoring & Rally

  1. Matches use rally scoring to 21 points (win by 2, cap at 30).
  2. You score a point on every rally, regardless of who served.
